Knowing how to write a good post or article isn't enough: what builds a presence
is **consistency over time.** A brand is recognised by its voice, its rhythm, its
look. This closing lesson helps you hold a line — without spending your evenings
on it.

## The key idea

<Idee>
Consistency doesn't come from a big brand document, but from **a few simple rules
you give back to the AI each time.** Three or four sentences about your tone, your
words, what you never say — and your content starts to resemble itself, whatever
the topic. That's your "voice", and it fits on a sticky note.
</Idee>

## Writing your brand voice (in a few lines)

No theory needed. Answer three questions and keep the answers:

- **Tone** — how you speak. *"Direct, warm, never preachy."*
- **Words** — the ones you use, the ones you ban. *"We say 'you', no jargon, no
  superlatives."*
- **What you stand for** — the idea behind your work. *"A beautiful garden made
  accessible, not a luxury."*

<Exemple>
Paste these rules at the top of every request: *"Here's my brand voice: [3 lines].
Write this post respecting it."* The AI aligns immediately, and all your content
gains a family resemblance.
</Exemple>

## Holding a realistic rhythm

<Attention>
Regularity beats intensity. Most online presences fade not from a lack of ideas,
but from an **unsustainable rhythm**: you publish daily for two weeks, then
nothing. Better **one piece a week, sustained for a year,** than ten at once. Set
a rhythm you can keep even on a busy week.
</Attention>

The good habit: **prepare ahead.** When you have a quiet hour, produce three or
four pieces from your stock of angles (lesson 1) and your templates (module M2).
On tight weeks, you draw from the reserve instead of creating under pressure.

## Visual consistency

If you use AI-generated images, the same principle applies: consistency comes
from **repeated constants.** Fix two or three invariants — a style ("bright,
natural photo"), a mood, a palette — and give them back at every generation.

<Idee>
A visual doesn't need to be spectacular, it needs to be **recognisable.** Three
images that share the same mood beat one stunning image and three others in a
different style. And stay honest: a visual shouldn't promise what you don't
deliver.
</Idee>

## Your turn

<Exercice>
Write your **brand voice in 3 lines** (tone, words, what you stand for). Then test
it: take an angle from your stock and have a post generated **twice** — once
without your rules, once with. Compare: the "with" version should already sound
more like you.

Keep these 3 lines with your templates: from now on, it's the first ingredient of
every content request you make.
</Exercice>

## In short

- Consistency comes down to **a few voice rules** given back at every request,
  not a big document.
- Define **tone + words + what you stand for**, and keep them at hand.
- Aim for a **regular, sustainable rhythm**; **prepare ahead** in the quiet
  times.
- For **visuals**, repeat two or three constants: recognisable beats spectacular.

<Formateur>
**Run-through (≈ 15 min).** 2 min module synthesis (ideas → posts → articles →
line) · 4 min on the 3-line brand voice, written live · 3 min on rhythm and
preparing ahead · 3 min on visuals · 3 min comparative exercise.

**Landing the module.** Everyone should leave with their **3 lines of brand
voice** written during the session. Do the "before/after" test on screen on a
participant's trade: seeing a post become "theirs" thanks to three rules is the
best argument for consistency.

**Connecting the modules.** This is where it all ties together: the stock of
angles (lesson 1), the templates (M2) and the brand voice form a small production
system. Naming it explicitly helps the group see the whole, not isolated tricks.

**Frequent questions**
- *"Do I have to be everywhere (all networks)?"* → no. Better one channel held
  with regularity than five abandoned ones. Choose where your clients are.
- *"What if I have no visual talent?"* → repeated constants are enough for honest
  consistency; no need to aim for studio quality. Regularity does the rest.

**Pitfall to avoid:** don't push to industrialise production to the point of
losing authenticity. The thread running through the programme stays the M1
mindset: use AI to save time, without giving up what makes you singular.
</Formateur>
