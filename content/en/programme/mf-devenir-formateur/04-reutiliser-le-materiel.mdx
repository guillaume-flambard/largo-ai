You have the posture, the facilitation, the group management. Final step: make the
material your own to turn it into **your** sessions. The Largo lessons aren't a
script to recite, but raw material to adapt to each group. This closing lesson
shows you how.

## The key idea

<Idee>
The Largo material is a **base to adapt**, not a fixed text. Your value as a
trainer lies precisely in the adjustment: choosing the right lessons, replacing
generic examples with ones from your participants' trade, calibrating the depth.
The same content, made relevant for THIS group.
</Idee>

## Composing a tailored flow

You don't have to cover everything in order. Depending on time and audience, pick
and choose:

<Exemple>
- **Half-day discovery**: all of M1 (the posture), then one lesson of M2 for a
  first concrete result.
- **Marketing group**: M1 (reflexes) + all of M3 (content) + M5 lesson 1 (data).
- **Executives**: M1 + an overview of M4 (automation) + M5 (compliance as a
  strategic issue).

The modular structure is made for this: you assemble according to the need.
</Exemple>

## Adapting examples to the trade

<Attention>
The greatest attention-killer is the generic example that doesn't speak to the
group. A carpenter's example falls flat in front of accountants. **Before each
session**, spot the dominant sector and replace two or three examples with cases
from their world — the "For the trainer" zones explicitly invite you to. Also ask
for their cases at the start of the session: those are the best.
</Attention>

## Passing it on in the Largo spirit

<Idee>
Adapt, yes; betray the spirit, no. Whatever you change, keep the programme's
thread: the **posture** (use then verify, stay in control, remain responsible) and
**honesty** (don't oversell, also show AI's limits). It's this integrity that
makes a Largo trainer's value — and reputation. Promising miracles hurts AI as
much as you.
</Idee>

## Keeping your material alive

Like your templates (M2) and your assistants (M4), your trainer material
**grows richer with use.** After each session, note what worked, the example
that landed, the recurring question. From session to session, you build a personal
repertoire that makes each run smoother than the last.

## Your turn

<Exercice>
Prepare **a complete session** for an audience you know (real or imagined).
Concretely:

1. **Choose** the lessons (and the order) according to their need and the time
   available.
2. **Adapt** two or three examples to their sector.
3. **Identify** where you'll have them practise on their real cases.

You've got your first tailored session — and the method for all the ones to come.
Welcome to the ranks of Largo trainers.
</Exercice>

## In short

- The material is a **base to adapt**, not a script: the adjustment is what makes
  your value.
- You **compose a tailored flow** by picking lessons according to audience and
  time.
- You **adapt the examples** to the group's trade (and take their real cases).
- You stay true to the **Largo spirit**: posture and honesty, never the oversell.
